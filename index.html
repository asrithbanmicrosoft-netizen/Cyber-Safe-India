Cyber Safety Tips

Use Strong and Unique Passwords: Create passwords with at least 12 characters, mixing letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid reusing the same password across accounts.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Even if your password is stolen, 2FA keeps attackers out by requiring a second verification step.

Protect Your Personal Information: Never share banking details or addresses on unknown sites. Always check for ( https:// ) before entering info.

Stay Alert Against Phishing: Donâ€™t click suspicious links or emails. Verify sender details before sharing any information.

Keep Devices Updated: Regularly install security patches on phones, computers, and apps to block vulnerabilities.

Use Safe Wi-Fi Connections: Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking. Use a VPN for extra safety when browsing outside home.

Install Security Software: Antivirus and firewalls protect against hidden threats. Keep them updated regularly.

Think Before You Share: Oversharing on social media can expose you to scams. Review your privacy settings often.

Be Careful with Downloads: Only install apps from trusted sources. Pirated files often carry malware.

Report Suspicious Activities: If you face cyberbullying or fraud, report immediately to cyber helplines or authorities.
